About Us

This web-app helps you quickly analyze a skin disease. This web-app does it in an instant!

This web-app is designed to be more than a one-disease app. We have 10,015 test images and growing. We are beginning trials to review our accuracy as a diagnostic aid for skin diseases.

Our aim is to provide a simple, inexpensive software system to identify potentially all types of skin diseases using a web-app-based platform.

Our system uses Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) algorithms to detect skin-disease types.


We are currently traning and testing our datasets and algorithm to achieve more accuracy and reliable models. As our machine learning learns, this web-app will recognize more and more skin diseases.

How it Works

The Algorithm

This skin disease analyzer uses Convolutional Neural Network, in creating the model, the test images gone through data preparations and be fed to tensorflow keras for the web deployment. The model to identify the skin disease has an accuracy of 81%. This web-application supports the 81% accuracy by displaying 3 possible skin disease, the top 2 diagnosis has an accuracy of 91% while the top 3 diagnosis has an accuracy of 96%. By that being said, we therefore conclude that the model is quite good in detecting skin diseases, but the outcome of skin diagnose comparisons is only effective when discussed with a qualified health care provider

The Dataset

Our reference image Dataset is a collected dermatoscopic images from different populations, acquired and stored by different modalities.

The final dataset consists of 10,015 dermatoscopic images which can serve as a training set for academic machine learning purposes. Cases include a representative collection of all important diagnostic categories in the realm of pigmented lesions: Actinic keratoses and Intraepithelial carcinoma / Bowen's disease (akiec), Basal cell carcinoma (bcc), Benign keratosis-like lesions (solar lentigines / seborrheic keratoses and lichen-planus like keratoses, bkl), dermatofibroma (df), melanoma (mel), melanocytic nevi (nv) and vascular lesions (angiomas, angiokeratomas, pyogenic granulomas and hemorrhage, vasc)..

More than 50% of lesions are confirmed through histopathology (histo), the ground truth for the rest of the cases is either follow-up examination (followup), expert consensus (consensus), or confirmation by in-vivo confocal microscopy (confocal).

FAQs

Will I need to wait for the model to download every time I use the web-app?

No. Once the model downloads it will be cached in memory. The next time you visit this site you won’t need to wait.

What about patient data privacy?

Your images are never sent to an external server. Any image you submit stays on your computer or mobile phone. This is because the model is running on your device. This is also why a batch of images can be analyzed in less than two seconds.

The tech that makes this possible is called Tensorflow.js. It was created by Google.

What do the decimal numbers mean?

They are probability scores between 0 and 1. They show how confident the model is that a particular condition is present on the image.
For example, mel, Melanoma: 0.231 means that the model is 23% sure that the skin disease shown in the image is melanoma.

How do I use this app on a computer?

- Go to this site on your computer.
- Login an account. (If don't have an existing account, you can signup)
- Click the black button.
- Select one image and click ‘Open’.
- The app will print results for each image.
